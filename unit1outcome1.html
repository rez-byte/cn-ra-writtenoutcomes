1.1 Describe the purpose of IT in modern business

Today, the implementation of IT into modern business models, is almost a prerequisite and something is seen as required for any full-fledged business to succeed. You can see this a-lot in the services industries, and now even more so in essential industries such as hospitals, pharmacies, municipal councils, banks, post offices and more.

And while it may not have occurred to a lot of older business models, until after the fact, we now live in a digital age where almost all consumers and products are interconnected online, primarily through their own mobile devices. And any business or service that isn't will always lose out on this ever-growing market.

One of the largest adopters of the IT business model would be retailers who now run online marketplaces, which are available to a customer 24/7, shops that literally never sleep. This kind of business not only increase the sales for a physical store locations be it a boutique or a large chains but in certain instances (like in the case for Amazon) completely negates the need for one. There is also the case to be made for customers who quite often have difficulty going in to stores be it either because a physical difficulty of reaching said location to even customers who have trouble in public suffer from anxiety around crowds or suffer from Agoraphobia, being able to consider these kinds of conditions and now cater to them will open new market revenues that previously your business wouldn’t have been able to access otherwise. Lets of course not forget the current Pandemic, where in the government has set many new conditions and regulations on physical stores to be safer which negatively impact sales , not to mention customers trying to be safe who simply won’t risk going inside a high traffic area, and online presence will allow for you business to continue sales without worry.

During this pandemic for example the business models that succeeded most were primarily or solely IT focused ones that centred around delivering a service to the comfort of one homes such as the exercise bike company ‘Peloton’ who’s sudden sales and increased memberships increased its stock prices so much they ended up getting reported on in the news and food delivery services like ‘Deliveroo’ or ‘Just Eat’ all of which equally increased in use during early lockdown periods, where some people couldn’t leave their homes at all. And even an instance wherein a company seemingly doesn't need to utilise any digital features, such as old-fashioned tailor shop. There will always be room for information technologies in the back-end of your business be it through book-keeping, online banking or more simply to connect with new and old clients.

1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects

Naturally, digital projects carry risks with them just any other project could. Though some are not the same, for example poor or a lack of internet connectivity could interrupt a project’s chance successfulness. In most work environments loss of internet access could stall communication between two developers who might be working to together to write a piece of code, and whilst they might be able to complete their own individuals tasks alone, they would still need access to the internet to share their code with one another.

Also since most digital projects require the use of computer of some kind any hardware failures and/or a computer restart, could lead to a loss of an unsaved project or piece code, which in term could lead to great loss of time that could cause a team to miss their deadlines. Most of these kinds issues of course can be solved by subscribing to a most internet providers business plan’s which would provide a more stable internet connection and better service for when you might drop a connection allowing a quicker resolution to the situation, and the use of cloud software to regularly save and upload projects off-site will allow for all your data to be protected in the case of computer failure. But these aren’t the only risk on could take, people will still need to wary of human risks in the work environment for example whilst working at home, a child could accidentally access and delete your software and even an office space you need to aware of the security of your building and the access to the outside could somebody just walk and take your information?

Any member of a digital project or company should be adequately made aware of the risks and trained against social engineering tricks and the like, to keep them safe and your project or company information private. Also a general rule of thumb most people utilising out of date software on their computers are simply more vulnerable to external intrusion and you can very easily negate this by keeping your computers always up to date and using the correct firewalls and necessary anti-virus protections to keep you safe.

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects

As the digital market is still a new and growing industry their are many new and changing guidelines when it comes to running your business. And there are multiple organisations and .gov listed websites that maintain a detailed and constantly updated record of all these guidelines, that are dedicated to sharing all the latest news and changes within the laws with you.

Some of the more prevalent issues right now for example would be the implementation of GDPR laws in the U.K, A lot of digital and online projects will be directly affected by this since everyone on the internet basically uses and processes one an others personal data. Be it in automated function such as filing a form on a website to a profile that someone maintains on social media.

The ICO maintains seven principles for GDPR;
- Lawfulness, fairness and transparency
- Purpose limitation
- Data minimisation
- Accuracy
- Storage limitation
- Integrity and confidentiality (security)
- Accountability

These principles essentially allow people to help maintain the rights to their information and how this information gets used the simplest example, of this would be the right to erasure; which essentially means that any one person can request that a website delete all the personal information they have stored on their website. With these guidelines being implemented, this means that if your business was based on using the personal data clients give you then you need to tell them exactly how you would use this information and in the event that they asked to know how this information is used or for access to a copy they would get it, businesses that don’t comply to these new standards would be then fined and even possibly prosecuted depending on the severity of the violation.
